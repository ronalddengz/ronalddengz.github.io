PATRICK

What doth man?
Such a question has perplexed physcians, anthropologists, and sociologists alike for the past four and a half millenia. I sought to tackle this forlorn inquiry myself and in my studies of lines, colors, and the like, I have stumbled upon a form which best depicts the state of, or preception of from others around it, man.
Question no more "What doth man?" any longer, for I have seen the face of man and I have drawn him. I present to you, dear viewer, a collection of a selection of 16 portraitures depicting man and what man doth.

Behold!
